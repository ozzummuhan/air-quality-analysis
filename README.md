# air-quality-analysis
Analysis of PM2.5 pollution and its relationship with GDP
Air Quality vs Economic Development (2023)
Overview
This project analyzes the relationship between air pollution (PM2.5) and GDP per capita across 9 countries.
Objective
To understand whether economic growth leads to better air quality.
Tools Used
- Excel
- Statistical Analysis (T-test, Correlation, Regression)
Key Findings
- Strong negative correlation (-0.79) between GDP and pollution
- Economic factors explain 63% of pollution variation
- Significant regional differences (p = 0.012)
Insights
Higher-income countries tend to have cleaner air due to:
- Better regulations
- Cleaner technologies
- Environmental awareness
Files
- Dataset
- Analysis (Excel)
- Report (PDF)
 Conclusion
Economic growth plays a critical role in improving air quality, but global inequality remains a major challenge.
